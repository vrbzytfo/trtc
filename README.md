## What Does Tencent RTC Actually Do?

It's a suite of products, not a single tool. Depending on what you're building, you'd pick the right one:

**Call** — for 1-on-1 and small group video/audio calls (think a telehealth consultation app, a dating platform, or a tutoring session). Comes with prebuilt UI you can drop straight in.

**Conference** — when you need Zoom-style meeting rooms, with up to 100 participants, room management, and all the meeting-room trimmings.

**Live** — interactive live streaming with audience participation, gifting, PK battles. The social entertainment crowd loves this one — WeSing, the karaoke app with 150 million downloads, runs on it.

**Chat** — full messaging SDK for in-app chat. Not just text, but group management, message history, content moderation hooks, all of it.

**RTC Engine** — the low-level SDK for developers who want to build something custom. If you have specific UI requirements or unusual streaming scenarios, this gives you the most flexibility.

**Beauty AR** — real-time face filters, skin smoothing, AR stickers. Useful if you're building anything in the social or entertainment space.

**In-game Voice Chat (GVoice)** — voice channels for games, with speech-to-text, real-time translation, and the kind of low-latency that actually matters when you're mid-match.

**Conversational AI** — the newest addition, built for AI voice agents with ultra-low latency and smart interruption handling. They recently partnered with Cartesia to bring lifelike voice synthesis into the mix.

The underlying network spans 30,000+ endpoints worldwide, maintains end-to-end latency under 300ms, and promises 99.99% uptime. Over 9,000 businesses run on it, and the platform handles more than 3 billion call minutes daily.
